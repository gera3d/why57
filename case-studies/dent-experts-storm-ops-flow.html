The Problem

Running a national PDR operation on tools built for the carriers.

Dent Experts runs a national paintless dent repair and hail restoration business — a vertical where speed, claim accuracy, and real-time coordination between field crews, adjusters, and insurance carriers determines whether you win or lose a storm event. When a hailstorm hits, the window to mobilize, inspect, and submit is measured in days, not weeks.

The problem: the dominant platforms in this space were built by the insurance companies, for the insurance companies. They're designed to slow the operator down and funnel data back to the carrier on the carrier's terms. Dent Experts was using tools that weren't built for how their operation actually runs — and it showed in their workflow.

They needed their own system. A custom iPhone app their field crews could use on-site during inspections, a backend that connected directly to the insurance platforms they work with every day, and a Storm Ops Flow engine that tracked every job from first contact through final payment — without the bottlenecks baked into someone else's software.

What We Built

A custom iPhone app and a complete ops platform.

We built Dent Experts a custom iOS app and a connected Storm Ops Flow platform — designed together from the start so the field experience and the back-office experience are one system, not two tools trying to talk to each other.

The iPhone App

The iPhone app is what the field crews use on-site. It pulls their active job queue, surfaces the inspection workflow for each vehicle, and lets techs capture photos that are immediately tied to the right claim record — no emailing photos to someone in the office, no re-entering VINs, no lost paperwork. The app talks directly to the backend in real time, so the moment a tech marks an inspection complete, it moves in the system. Built for the way field work actually happens: one hand, on a phone, in a parking lot after a storm.

Storm Ops Flow

Storm Ops Flow is the workflow engine sitting behind the iPhone app. It tracks every job through the full lifecycle — storm identification, vehicle dispatch, inspection, documentation, claim submission, scheduling, and final repair sign-off. Each stage has its own status, assignment, and audit trail. Nothing falls through the cracks because the system owns the handoffs, not the people.

Insurance Platform Integration

The major insurance platforms Dent Experts works with daily aren't going away — so we built direct integrations with them. Claim data flows in and out automatically. No manual re-entry across systems, no copying data from one portal into another. The Storm Ops platform is the source of truth; the carrier platforms are connected endpoints.

Management Dashboard

Behind the field app, management gets a live view of the entire operation — active jobs by crew, claim status by carrier, photos and documentation on demand, and pipeline visibility across storm events. The kind of visibility that used to require three separate carrier logins and a spreadsheet.

  • Custom iPhone app — built for field crews doing inspections on-site
  • Real-time photo capture tied directly to claim records
  • Storm Ops Flow — full job lifecycle from dispatch through payment
  • Direct integration with major insurance carrier platforms
  • Management dashboard with live pipeline and claim visibility
  • Designed for national deployment across multiple storm markets

Their own platform. Built for the operator, not the carrier.

Dent Experts now runs their Storm Ops on a platform built specifically for how they work. The iPhone app is in the hands of their field crews. The workflow engine tracks every job through every stage. The integrations feed data directly to and from the insurance carriers — on Dent Experts' terms, not the carriers'.
