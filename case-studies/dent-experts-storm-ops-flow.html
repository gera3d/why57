The Problem

Running a PDR operation across disconnected handoffs.

Dent Experts operates in paintless dent repair and hail restoration, where field crews, adjusters, office staff, and insurance systems all touch the same claim. Delays or missing context at any handoff create avoidable rework.

The tools available to the operator reflected carrier requirements more than Dent Experts' field workflow. The team needed a system shaped around how inspections, documentation, and internal decisions actually moved.

They needed their own system. A custom iPhone app their field crews could use on-site during inspections, a backend that connected directly to the insurance platforms they work with every day, and a Storm Ops Flow engine that tracked every job from first contact through final payment — without the bottlenecks baked into someone else's software.

The Intervention

A custom iPhone app and a complete ops platform.

We built Dent Experts a custom iOS app and a connected Storm Ops Flow platform — designed together from the start so the field experience and the back-office experience are one system, not two tools trying to talk to each other.

The iPhone App

The iPhone app supports an active job queue, the inspection workflow for each vehicle, and photo capture tied to the correct claim record. The design keeps field context connected to the backend so the office can work from the same record.

Storm Ops Flow

Storm Ops Flow is the workflow engine sitting behind the iPhone app. It tracks every job through the full lifecycle — storm identification, vehicle dispatch, inspection, documentation, claim submission, scheduling, and final repair sign-off. Each stage has its own status, assignment, and audit trail. Nothing falls through the cracks because the system owns the handoffs, not the people.

Insurance Platform Integration

The carrier platforms Dent Experts works with remain part of the process, so the Storm Ops platform was designed with integration points for claim data. The goal was to reduce redundant handling while keeping the operator's record central.

Management Dashboard

Behind the field app, management gets a live view of the entire operation — active jobs by crew, claim status by carrier, photos and documentation on demand, and pipeline visibility across storm events. The kind of visibility that used to require three separate carrier logins and a spreadsheet.

  • Custom iPhone app — built for field crews doing inspections on-site
  • Real-time photo capture tied directly to claim records
  • Storm Ops Flow — full job lifecycle from dispatch through payment
  • Direct integration with major insurance carrier platforms
  • Management dashboard with live pipeline and claim visibility
  • Designed to support multiple crews and storm markets

The Result

Their own platform. Built for the operator, not the carrier.

The delivered product connected the field inspection experience to a shared operations record and management view. That is the implementation outcome supported by the portfolio record.
