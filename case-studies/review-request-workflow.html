The Problem

Positive service moments. No consistent follow-up.

The portfolio record concerns an insurance marketplace where customer conversations happened through individual agents. It describes inconsistent review follow-up and limited management visibility as the starting problem.

That made follow-up inconsistent and management visibility limited. An agent could deliver a strong experience without an easy, attributable path for the customer to share it publicly.

The operational problem was clear: make the request timely and personal, keep the destination tied to the correct agent, and give managers a useful view of participation without turning follow-up into another manual task.

The Intervention

A proposed workflow — documented without overstating delivery.

The portfolio record describes an agent-specific review workflow that informed the 57Seconds product. It covers landing pages, post-interaction messaging, and management reporting. The repository does not contain an accepted scope or client sign-off confirming which components reached production.

Agent-Specific Landing Pages

The documented design used agent-specific landing pages with profile information and a direct review link, so a request could lead to an agent-specific destination rather than a generic page.

Automated SMS Workflows

The documented workflow paired an eligible customer interaction with an SMS containing the relevant review link. The repository does not verify trigger coverage, sending identity, timing, delivery rate, or response-rate impact.

Management Dashboard

A review request became a managed workflow.
