Case Study · Health for California · Insurance

A repeatable review request. For every agent.

Health for California needed a consistent way to ask for feedback after customer interactions and understand adoption across its agent network.

The Problem

Positive service moments. No consistent follow-up.

Health for California connects insurance agents with people shopping for coverage. Customer conversations happened through individual agents, but the organization did not have one repeatable workflow for asking for a review at the right moment.

That made follow-up inconsistent and management visibility limited. An agent could deliver a strong experience without an easy, attributable path for the customer to share it publicly.

The operational problem was clear: make the request timely and personal, keep the destination tied to the correct agent, and give managers a useful view of participation without turning follow-up into another manual task.

The Intervention

57Seconds — built specifically for HFC.

We built an end-to-end review acquisition engine — what became the foundation of our 57Seconds product. Every piece of the system was designed around one core insight: the best moment to ask for a review is immediately after the customer interaction, while the positive feeling is still fresh.

Agent-Specific Landing Pages

Every agent on the HFC platform got their own custom landing page — a digital profile showcasing their reviews, their credentials, and a direct link to leave a Google review. When a client clicked through from the SMS, they arrived at a page that felt personal, not generic.

Automated SMS Workflows

After each customer enrollment or service call, an automated SMS was triggered — sent from the agent's own line, timed for maximum response rate. The message was short, personal, and included a direct link to the agent's review page. No chasing. No awkward asks. The system did it automatically.

Management Dashboard

Management needed visibility, not just volume. We built a live Google Sheets dashboard tracking every agent's review velocity, response rates, and coaching flags — so managers could identify which agents were thriving and which needed support.

  • Custom landing page per agent with individual review link
  • Automated post-interaction SMS triggered from agent number
  • Management dashboard: per-agent review velocity and trends
  • Google Sheets integration for real-time reporting
  • Message timing and copy that could be tested and refined

The Result

A review request became a managed workflow.

The implementation connected the post-interaction ask, the agent-specific destination, and management reporting. That replaced an ad hoc activity with a repeatable process the organization could observe and refine.
