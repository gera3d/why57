The Problem

Positive service moments. No consistent follow-up.

Health for California’s agents were helping real people navigate an important, high-consideration decision. The work was personal, but the online reputation system was nearly invisible: the organization had roughly ten Google reviews, and individual agents had little or no public proof of the service they were delivering.

The issue wasn’t customer satisfaction. It was the handoff. A strong conversation ended, the customer moved on, and the moment where they were most likely to leave a review disappeared with it.

The operational problem was clear: make the request timely and personal, keep the destination tied to the correct agent, and give managers a useful view of participation without turning follow-up into another manual task.

What We Built

A review workflow that feels personal at scale.

57 built the system that became 57Seconds: a connected path from an eligible customer interaction to an agent-specific review destination, with a management view that made participation visible.

Agent-Specific Landing Pages

Every agent received a tailored landing page with their own profile and direct review path. Customers landed in a place that reflected the person who had actually helped them—not a generic corporate request.

Automated SMS Workflows

Follow-up messages were tied to the right customer moment and the right agent, making review requests consistent without asking agents to manually chase every interaction.

Management Dashboard

A shared reporting view gave leadership a practical way to see activity and momentum across the network, then use that signal to improve the workflow over time.

  • Agent-specific landing pages with individual review links
  • Automated SMS follow-up tied to the customer journey
  • Management reporting for agent-level activity and trends
  • Google Sheets integration for lightweight operational visibility
  • Message timing and copy that could be tested and refined

The Result

A review request became a managed workflow.

Over four years, Health for California grew from roughly ten reviews to more than 9,000 across its agent network. The result was more than a larger number: agents gained visible reputation, managers gained a repeatable operating system, and 57 gained the foundation for a product we still use to help call-heavy teams today.
