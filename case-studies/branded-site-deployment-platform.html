The Problem

Custom client sites. A coordination-heavy fulfillment process.

The portfolio record concerns a digital marketing agency producing sites for dental and oral-surgery practices. Each site combined practice-specific branding, video, and content aligned with the practice's specialty and geography.

The recorded starting point involved repeated design handoffs, content assembly, WordPress configuration, video integration, and QA. The portfolio describes coordination as a constraint, but the repository does not contain a time study or throughput baseline.

The documented opportunity was to standardize repeatable setup work while keeping brand and content decisions intentionally custom.

The Intervention

A deployment platform — not a shortcut.

The documented design used reusable components and shared setup steps while preserving site-specific brand and content choices. The repository does not verify comparative quality or production speed.

Modular Site Architecture

The portfolio record describes a modular architecture of configurable components intended to support differences in practice size, specialty, location, and brand. It does not establish the number of production configurations or the scope accepted by the client.

4K Video Integration Engine

The documented workflow included handling for on-site video, including encoding, compression, responsive delivery, and placement. The repository does not verify that every step ran automatically or without human intervention.

Automated Configuration & QA

The portfolio record describes repeatable setup and checks for domains, SEO defaults, analytics, forms, and mobile QA, with exceptions intended for human review. It does not establish one-click operation, complete automation, or production coverage.
