Scope tiers

The price follows the operational surface area.

These are scope patterns, not prebuilt packages. Every accepted build is quoted inside the same $5,000–$25,000 fixed-price policy.

Focused workflow

Remove a specific bottleneck

Best when a stable, painful workflow can be improved without rebuilding the rest of the operation.

  • Workflow rules and exception paths
  • A focused interface or automation
  • Necessary data handoffs
  • Launch handoff and measurement plan
Connected operations

Make existing systems work together

Best when the tools are usable but people still carry data, approvals, and status between them.

  • Cross-system workflow design
  • Integrations and shared data flow
  • Exception and status visibility
  • Validation and operating handoff
Internal operations tool

Give the workflow its own system

Best when the process is business-specific enough that another generic subscription will keep creating workarounds.

  • Core operational views
  • Roles and permissions
  • Reporting and control
  • Ownership and launch handoff

What shapes price

Complexity is not the feature count. It is the number of operational edges.

The quote gets clearer when the workflow, data, ownership, and finished outcome are concrete.

Workflow

Breadth and exceptions

More roles, decision paths, approvals, and edge cases create more behavior to define and verify.

Systems

Integrations and data

APIs, sync rules, migration, ownership, and failure recovery shape the work behind the interface.

Access

Identity and permissions

Internal users, clients, vendors, and administrators may need different views and authority.

Operation

Reporting and launch needs

Monitoring, audit records, dashboards, support ownership, and launch readiness belong in the scope.
