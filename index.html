Mutual Fit

Some projects move fast. Some should wait.

The best engagements start with a clear bottleneck, an owner on your side, and a first version that's worth shipping now.

Good fit

  • You can point to a real bottleneck costing time, revenue, or trust.
  • You want a sharp first release, not a giant 12-month rewrite.
  • Someone on your team can own feedback and decisions during the build.
  • You care about outcomes, not just buying dev hours.

Not yet

  • The problem is still vague and changes every time we describe it.
  • You want a bargain-bin build before validating the workflow.
  • No one can own the project internally once it goes live.
  • You need a generalist freelancer more than a scoped systems partner.

If this sounds like your situation, an initial call can clarify whether there is enough information to define a focused first version.

Common Questions

Straight answers. No fluff.

Do you work with businesses outside of Sonoma County? +

Coverage and working format depend on the engagement. Confirm both in the written scope before work begins.

What does a custom software project typically cost? +

Cost depends on scope, integrations, and delivery risk. A responsible quote requires a defined deliverable, known dependencies, and explicit assumptions.

How long does a project take to ship? +

Timeline depends on scope, dependencies, and access to the systems involved. We agree on milestones before work starts and prioritize a useful first release.

Do you integrate with Salesforce, QuickBooks, or Google Sheets? +

We can assess tools that offer supported APIs or webhooks. Exact feasibility, permissions, rate limits, and data availability are confirmed during discovery before an integration is promised.
