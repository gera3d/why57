Security and data boundary

Requirements start with the solicitation and the data.

Security obligations are confirmed against the applicable clauses, system boundary, data types, hosting model, access model, integrations, and responsible parties before scope is accepted.

Not represented on this page: CMMC certification, NIST SP 800-171 implementation, an SPRS score, FedRAMP authorization, FISMA compliance, an authority to operate, facility or personnel clearances, or any similar security status.

  • Identify whether the work involves public data, ordinary business information, FCI, CUI, or another regulated data type.
  • Define identity, permissions, logging, backup, recovery, incident, subcontractor, and data-disposal responsibilities.
  • Confirm hosting, third-party services, supported APIs, access limits, and ownership before committing to an integration.
  • Document acceptance, release, training, operational ownership, and post-release support in the applicable contract documents.

Submission boundary: Do not send controlled information, credentials, access tokens, production data, or sensitive records through this website or ordinary email.
